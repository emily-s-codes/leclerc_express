Marc-André Leclerc, alpinist

Marc-André Leclerc's passion for climbing began at a young age, with a book and a shopping mall climbing wall. He soon progressed to self-taught outdoor climbing in the Canadian Cascade Range. Doggedly independent, he embarked on solo overnight climbing and camping adventures before joining the BC Mountaineering Club and quickly moving on to win both age-group and national competitions.

Sailing past the standards set by previous climbers, Leclerc notoriously soloed on devastatingly challenging peaks in Patagonia, which is beautifully documented in the 2021 film The Alpinist.

Leclerc blogged about his experiences, reminiscing often on the psychological and physical challenges of free solo climbing and sharing photos of daring moments and difficult terrain. From his writing we know that he took an open spiritual view of the mountains.

In July 2018, Leclerc and his sometimes-climbing partner Ryan Johnson lost their lives descending from a climb of the Mendenhall Towers. Once again the climb broke boundaries, as the two followed a new route up the North Face. Their bodies were traced to their decent ropes and are buried beneath an avalanche there to this day.
